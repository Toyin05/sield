COMMERCIAL LEASE AGREEMENT

THIS LEASE AGREEMENT is entered into on this 1st day of January 2024, between:

LANDLORD: Lagos Properties and Development Ltd, a company incorporated in Nigeria with RC No. 1234567, whose registered office is situated at 10 Adeola Odeku Street, Victoria Island, Lagos (hereinafter referred to as "the Landlord")

AND

TENANT: TechStart Solutions Nigeria Ltd, a company incorporated in Nigeria with RC No. 8765432, whose registered office is situated at Plot 789, Yaba Tech Park, Lagos (hereinafter referred to as "the Tenant")

BACKGROUND AND CONTEXT

In Nigeria's thriving tech ecosystem, this lease agreement supports the growth of local innovation. The Landlord owns a modern commercial property in the heart of Lagos, while the Tenant represents the new generation of Nigerian tech entrepreneurs who are building solutions for African markets.

The property is located in Victoria Island, one of Lagos' premier business districts, where many of Nigeria's leading tech companies have established their headquarters. This location provides the Tenant with access to reliable power supply, good internet connectivity, and proximity to major financial institutions.

1. PROPERTY DESCRIPTION AND LEASE TERMS

1.1 The Landlord hereby leases to the Tenant Suite 305, Victoria Plaza, No. 45 Adeola Odeku Street, Victoria Island, Lagos (hereinafter referred to as "the Premises").

1.2 The lease term begins on February 1, 2024, and expires on January 31, 2027, unless terminated earlier as provided in this agreement.

1.3 The Premises consist of approximately 2,500 square feet of modern office space on the third floor, with a stunning view of Lagos Lagoon and easy access to the building's conference facilities.

1.4 This location is ideal for a growing tech company, being just 10 minutes drive from Muritala Mohammed International Airport and surrounded by banks, restaurants, and other tech companies.

2. RENT PAYMENT - HOW MUCH AND WHEN

2.1 The Tenant agrees to pay monthly rent of ₦1,250,000 (One Million Two Hundred and Fifty Thousand Naira) payable in advance on or before the 1st day of each month.

2.2 The rent shall be paid by bank transfer to the Landlord's United Bank for Africa (UBA) account, account number 1234567890, or to such other account as the Landlord may notify in writing.

2.3 The first month’s rent of ₦1,250,000 plus a security deposit of ₦2,500,000 (equivalent to two months rent) are due before move-in on February 1, 2024.

2.4 Annual rent escalation of 10% will apply from year 2 onwards, reflecting Lagos market conditions and inflation rates.

3. BUSINESS USE - WHAT THE TENANT CAN USE IT FOR

3.1 The Premises shall be used for tech startup operations, including software development, marketing, business meetings, and related activities.

3.2 The Tenant is specifically permitted to:
   - Employ up to 25 staff members
   - Host client meetings and business events
   - Use the space for training and workshops
   - Operate servers and computer equipment (with proper cooling)
   - Display company branding and signage (subject to building approval)

3.3 The Tenant agrees to use the Premises in a professional manner that respects other tenants in the building.

4. UTILITIES AND SERVICES - POWER, WATER, INTERNET

4.1 The Landlord provides 24/7 power supply through a combination of NEPA grid connection and diesel generator backup. This is crucial in Lagos where power outages are common.

4.2 Air conditioning is available 24/7 through the building's centralized system, with backup generators ensuring continuous cooling.

4.3 The Tenant is responsible for:
   - Internet and telecommunications services (MTN, Airtel, or Glo business plans recommended)
   - Telephone line installation and monthly bills
   - Office equipment power consumption beyond building standard
   - Any additional computer servers or high-power equipment

4.4 Water supply is included in the rent, with backup borehole water system ensuring continuous supply even during Lagos water scarcity periods.

4.5 The building provides high-speed fiber internet as a shared service, but the Tenant should install dedicated business internet for reliability.

5. MAINTENANCE AND REPAIRS - WHO FIXES WHAT

5.1 The Landlord is responsible for:
   - Building structural integrity and roof repairs
   - Air conditioning system maintenance and repairs
   - Security system and building access controls
   - General building maintenance and common area upkeep
   - Elevator maintenance and repairs

5.2 The Tenant is responsible for:
   - Interior cleaning and daily office maintenance
   - Replacing fluorescent bulbs and basic electrical fixtures
   - Office furniture and equipment maintenance
   - Any damage caused by Tenant's staff or visitors
   - Pest control within the office space

5.3 The building employs a full-time maintenance team available 24/7 for emergencies, with response time under 30 minutes for urgent issues.

6. ALTERATIONS

6.1 The Tenant may make non-structural alterations to the interior of the Premises with the Landlord's prior written consent, which consent shall not be unreasonably withheld.

6.2 All alterations shall be performed in accordance with applicable building codes and regulations.

6.3 Upon termination of this lease, the Tenant shall have the option to remove alterations, or if the Landlord elects to retain them, they shall become the property of the Landlord.

7. ASSIGNMENT AND SUBLETTING

7.1 The Tenant shall not assign this lease or sublet the Premises without the prior written consent of the Landlord, which consent shall not be unreasonably withheld.

7.2 Any assignment or subletting shall not release the Tenant from liability under this lease.

8. INSURANCE - PROTECTION FOR EVERYONE

8.1 The Landlord maintains comprehensive building insurance covering fire, theft, and structural damage through leading Nigerian insurers like Leadway Assurance or AIICO Insurance.

8.2 The Tenant must maintain the following insurance policies:
   - Public liability insurance (minimum ₦50,000,000 coverage)
   - Fire and special perils insurance covering office equipment and furniture
   - Business interruption insurance
   - Employers liability insurance for all staff

8.3 All insurance policies must list "Lagos Properties and Development Ltd" as additional insured and must be with Nigerian insurance companies registered with NAICOM.

8.4 The Tenant must provide proof of insurance within 14 days of lease commencement and renewals must be submitted at least 30 days before expiry.

9. DEFAULT AND TERMINATION - WHAT HAPPENS IF THINGS GO WRONG

9.1 If the Tenant fails to pay rent for 2 consecutive months (giving consideration to Nigerian banking delays during festive periods), the Landlord may terminate the lease with 7 days written notice.

9.2 If the Tenant breaches any other terms and fails to remedy within 30 days after written notice, the Landlord may terminate the lease.

9.3 Upon termination, the Tenant must:
   - Pay all outstanding rent and utilities
   - Remove all personal belongings and equipment
   - Return all building access cards and keys
   - Leave the Premises in clean, good condition

9.4 The Landlord may enter the Premises after termination to prepare for new tenants, with reasonable notice to the former Tenant to retrieve any remaining items.

10. SECURITY DEPOSIT - PROTECTING THE LANDLORD'S INVESTMENT

10.1 The Tenant provides a security deposit of ₦2,500,000 (2 months rent) which will be held in an interest-bearing account with First Bank of Nigeria.

10.2 The deposit will be returned within 30 days after lease termination, less any deductions for:
   - Unpaid rent and utilities
   - Damage beyond normal wear and tear
   - Cleaning costs if the office is left in poor condition
   - Any other amounts owed under this lease

10.3 Interest earned on the security deposit will be shared equally between Landlord and Tenant.

11. LEGAL MATTERS - IMPORTANT LEGAL STUFF

11.1 This lease agreement is governed by the laws of the Federal Republic of Nigeria, specifically the Lagos State Tenancy Law and relevant Federal legislation.

11.2 Any disputes will first be addressed through mediation through the Lagos Multi-Door Court House, and if that fails, will be resolved in the Lagos State High Court.

11.3 All notices must be in writing and delivered by:
   - Certified mail with return receipt
   - Hand delivery with acknowledgment of receipt
   - Email with read receipt (for non-legal notices)

11.4 This lease agreement, along with any attachments, represents the complete agreement between the parties. Any changes must be made in writing and signed by both parties.

11.5 If any part of this agreement is found to be invalid, the rest of the agreement remains in effect.

SIGNATURES

Both parties have read and understood this agreement and sign voluntarily.
