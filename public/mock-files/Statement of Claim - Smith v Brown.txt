STATEMENT OF CLAIM

TO THE HONOURABLE COURT:

The Claimant, Adebayo Oluwaseun Smith, a software engineer residing at No. 45, Victoria Island, Lagos State, Nigeria, by this Statement of Claim, states as follows:

1. THE PARTIES

1.1 The Claimant is Adebayo Oluwaseun Smith, a software engineer and entrepreneur based in Victoria Island, Lagos.

1.2 The Defendant is Adunni Amoke Brown, a business consultant operating under the name "Brown Strategic Consulting" from Ikeja, Lagos State.

1.3 The Defendant is involved in marketing and business strategy consulting for SMEs in Lagos.

2. FACTUAL BACKGROUND - THE STORY BEHIND THE CASE

2.1 On or about March 15, 2023, both parties met at the Lagos StartUp Hub in Yaba during a networking event for tech entrepreneurs.

2.2 The Defendant approached the Claimant with a request to build a custom customer management system for her growing consulting business, which was expanding rapidly due to increased demand from Lagos SMEs.

2.3 On March 20, 2023, they signed a written contract (copy attached as Exhibit A) where the Claimant agreed to develop the software system within 6 months, with the Defendant paying ₦15,000,000 upon successful completion.

2.4 The Claimant worked diligently on the project, often working late nights and weekends to meet the deadline, even during the fuel scarcity crisis that affected Lagos in 2023.

2.5 The Claimant delivered the completed software system on September 25, 2023 - well within the agreed timeframe and even before the rainy season made transportation difficult.

2.6 The Defendant immediately started using the system for her business and even showcased it to potential clients as an example of her "tech-savvy" approach to consulting.

3. THE CLAIM - WHAT THE CLAIMANT IS ASKING FOR

3.1 Despite multiple reminders and even after the Defendant received a ₦2,000,000 government grant for her business expansion, she has refused to pay the ₦15,000,000 owed for the software development work.

3.2 The Defendant has given various excuses including "the system has bugs" and "my clients don't like it," but these are unfounded as the system was working perfectly and even helped her secure three major new clients.

3.3 The Claimant has suffered financial hardship as a result, having taken loans to finance the development work and being unable to pay staff salaries and office rent.

4. DETAILS OF THE CLAIM - WHAT THE CLAIMANT WANTS

4.1 The Claimant is seeking:
   - ₦15,000,000 for the unpaid software development contract
   - ₦3,000,000 in interest at 2% per month since October 2023
   - ₦2,000,000 for stress and reputational damage
   - Court costs and legal fees

4.2 This money is very important to the Claimant as it represents not just payment for work done, but also funding for the Claimant's startup to hire more developers and expand to serve other Nigerian businesses.

4.3 The Claimant believes this case will help set a precedent for protecting the rights of Nigerian tech entrepreneurs who provide services to businesses across the country.

5. VERIFICATION AND TRUTH

I, Adebayo Oluwaseun Smith, swear on the Holy Bible that the facts stated in this Statement of Claim are true and correct to the best of my knowledge. I understand that making false statements in court is a serious offense punishable under Nigerian law.
