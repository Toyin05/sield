POWER OF ATTORNEY

FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT AUTHORITY

I, MRS. GRACE Folasade Adebisi, of 34 Adeniran Ogunsanya Street, Surulere, Lagos State, Nigeria, hereby grant this Power of Attorney to:

MY SON: EMEKA CHUKWU ADEBISI, of 67 Borno Way, Ebute Metta, Lagos State, Nigeria (hereinafter referred to as "my Attorney").

BACKGROUND - WHY I NEED THIS

I am a 68-year-old retired civil servant who worked for 35 years in the Lagos State Ministry of Education. I have worked hard all my life, saved money, and invested wisely. Now, as I get older, I want to make sure my financial affairs are managed properly if I become unable to handle them myself.

I have confidence in my son's ability to handle these matters as he is a successful banker with First Bank of Nigeria and has always been responsible with money. He understands my values and will make decisions that would make me proud.

I want to ensure that:
- My bills get paid on time even if I'm ill
- My investments continue to grow
- My grandchildren's education fund is managed properly
- My small business (a provision shop) continues to operate
- I can afford good medical care when needed

Therefore, I give my Attorney the following powers:

1. GENERAL FINANCIAL POWERS

1.1 My Attorney is hereby authorized to:

(a) Buy, sell, lease, rent, or otherwise acquire or dispose of real property on my behalf;

(b) Manage and operate any business or commercial enterprise in which I have an interest;

(c) Buy, sell, or trade securities, bonds, stocks, mutual funds, and other investment instruments;

(d) Open, close, or manage bank accounts, savings accounts, investment accounts, and other financial accounts;

(e) Make deposits to and withdrawals from any financial accounts in my name;

(f) Borrow money on my behalf and pledge my assets as security for such loans;

(g) Purchase insurance policies and pay premiums on my behalf;

(h) Make investment decisions and manage my investment portfolio;

(i) Collect rents, royalties, dividends, interest, and other income due to me;

(j) Pay taxes, assessments, utilities, and other expenses on my behalf.

2. SPECIFIC BANK ACCOUNTS AND INVESTMENTS

2.1 Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, my Attorney is specifically authorized to:

(a) Access and manage my accounts at GTBank Surulere branch (Account ending 7890) - my pension savings
(b) Manage my current account at First Bank Ebute Metta (Account ending 1234) - for business operations
(c) Trade securities through my investment accounts with Nigerian Investment Securities Limited (NISL)
(d) Execute documents for my rental property at 45 Kajola Street, Mushin
(e) Make charitable donations on my behalf to my church (St. Paul Anglican Church, Surulere) and local charity
(f) Engage accountants and financial advisors, particularly my longtime accountant Mr. Wale Adeyemi
(g) Manage my Cooperative Savings and Credit Account with Lagos State Civil Service Cooperative
(h) Pay my monthly contributions to my children's university education fund

3. LIMITATIONS ON POWERS

3.1 My Attorney is NOT authorized to:

(a) Make gifts or donations of my assets without my written consent, except for gifts to family members made in the ordinary course of business;

(b) Create or modify any will, trust, or other estate planning document on my behalf;

(c) Make investment decisions that would be contrary to my previously expressed wishes or risk tolerance;

(d) Use my assets for any purpose other than my benefit and the benefit of my immediate family.

4. THIRD PARTY RELIANCE

4.1 Any third party who receives a copy of this Power of Attorney may rely upon its validity without further inquiry. I hereby ratify and confirm all acts that my Attorney may lawfully take in reliance on this Power of Attorney.

4.2 This Power of Attorney shall remain in effect until I revoke it in writing or until I become permanently incapacitated.

5. INDEMNIFICATION

5.1 I hereby indemnify and hold harmless my Attorney from any claims, damages, or expenses arising from the exercise of the powers granted herein, except in cases of gross negligence or willful misconduct.

6. COMPENSATION

6.1 My Attorney may receive reasonable compensation for services rendered under this Power of Attorney, to be paid from my assets.

7. EFFECTIVE DATE AND DURATION

7.1 This Power of Attorney shall become effective immediately upon execution and shall remain in effect until:

(a) I revoke it in writing; or

(b) I become permanently incapacitated and unable to manage my affairs; or

(c) My Attorney resigns or becomes unable to serve; or

(d) My death.

8. GOVERNING LAW AND LEGAL FRAMEWORK

8.1 This Power of Attorney shall be governed by the laws of the Federal Republic of Nigeria, particularly the Lagos State Power of Attorney Law and related Nigerian legislation.

8.2 This document is made in accordance with Nigerian legal requirements and shall be interpreted under Nigerian law.

SIGNATURE AND ATTESTATION
